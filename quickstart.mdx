This guide walks you through installing WP Manager Pro and completing your first key actions — checking site health, managing a plugin, enabling maintenance mode, and setting up two-factor authentication.

<Note>
You need Administrator access to your WordPress site to install and use WP Manager Pro. All REST API endpoints enforce the `manage_options` capability.
</Note>

## Get up and running

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the plugin">
    Download `wp-manager-pro-v2.9.2.zip` from the [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/nurkamol/wp-manager-pro/releases) page.

    In your WordPress admin panel, go to **Plugins → Add New → Upload Plugin**. Upload the ZIP file, click **Install Now**, then click **Activate Plugin**.

    <Tip>
      You can also install manually by unzipping to `wp-content/plugins/` and activating via the Plugins list.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open WP Manager Pro">
    After activation, look for **WP Manager** in the WordPress admin sidebar. Click it to open the control panel.

    You can also click the **Open** link in the Plugins list row next to WP Manager Pro.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check site health on the Dashboard">
    The **Dashboard** is the first page you'll see. It shows a real-time snapshot of your site:

    - Active plugin, theme, and user counts
    - Available updates for WordPress core, plugins, and themes
    - PHP memory limit, max execution time, and upload limit
    - Database size, uploads folder size, and disk usage
    - Recent posts summary

    If you see any update alerts, click through to **Plugin Manager** or **Theme Manager** to apply them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Activate or deactivate a plugin">
    Go to **Plugin Manager** in the sidebar. You'll see a list of all installed plugins with their activation status.

    - Click the toggle switch next to any plugin to activate or deactivate it instantly.
    - Click the update icon on any plugin with a pending update to apply it in one click.
    - Use the search bar at the top to filter plugins by name.

    <Tip>
      Click **Check Updates** (top-right) to force WordPress to re-query WordPress.org for fresh update data without a page reload.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable Maintenance Mode">
    Go to **Maintenance** in the sidebar.

    1. Click the **Content** tab and enter a title and message for your maintenance page.
    2. Click the **Appearance** tab and choose a gradient preset (e.g., Midnight, Sunset, Forest).
    3. Click **Enable Maintenance** to go live. All non-admin visitors will see your maintenance page.
    4. Click **Save Settings** to save your design without changing the active state.

    <Warning>
      While maintenance mode is active, all regular visitors are blocked from accessing the site. Only logged-in administrators see the normal site.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up Two-Factor Authentication">
    Go to **Security** in the sidebar, then click the **Two-Factor** tab.

    1. Click **Set Up Two-Factor Auth** to generate your TOTP secret.
    2. Scan the QR code with Google Authenticator, Authy, or any TOTP app.
    3. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app and click **Verify & Activate**.
    4. Save the 8 one-time backup codes shown — they are only displayed once.

    <Warning>
      Store your backup codes in a safe place. They are the only way to access your account if you lose access to your authenticator app.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>
